Official Profile

Religion: Christian — explicitly professed: 'I will choose God over humanism, our children and parents over indoctrination, and our brave law enforcement officers' (STARRS interview). Denomination not specified.
Background: Chandler Langevin — Brevard County native, Titusville High grad. ~10 years U.S. Navy Hospital Corpsman (FMF) attached to USMC — NATO Liaison with 4th ANGLICO in Afghanistan; sniper-platoon medic with 1st Bn 25th Marines in the Pacific. Took early honorable discharge over the Biden COVID vaccine mandate ('unwillingness to be a guinea pig'). Works in veteran advocacy; founded local youth program 'Florida Scouts'; State Director, American Restoration Coalition. Previously on Odyssey Charter School board (removed Oct 2025 over the censure controversy). Briefly filed for FL HD-33 in 2023 before withdrawing and endorsing Monique Miller. Active with Republican Liberty Caucus (Brevard chapter), Moms for Liberty (Brevard), and Space Coast Young Republicans. CENSURE / LITIGATION FACTS (recorded candidly per brand voice): On Oct 17, 2025 Palm Bay Council voted 3-2 to censure Langevin and ask Gov. DeSantis to suspend him under FS 112.51 after Langevin posted on X 'Deport every Indian immediately,' Confederate-soldier-praise posts including a description of Robert E. Lee as 'a Godly and honorable presence,' and a request on city letterhead asking President Trump to pardon white-nationalist activist Robert Rundo (founder of the Rise Above Movement). Langevin filed a federal First Amendment suit (Langevin v. Palm Bay) represented by Anthony Sabatini; bench trial began Jan 21, 2026.
