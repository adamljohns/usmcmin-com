Official Profile

Background: Shaniqua 'Shan' Rose. Orlando City Commissioner, District 5, since June 24, 2024 (took office via special runoff after Gov. DeSantis suspended prior Commissioner Regina Hill in April 2024 over elder-abuse/fraud allegations); re-elected Nov 4, 2025 defeating Hill ~52%-33% with Lawanna Gelser ~15%; current 4-year term runs to 2029. Officially nonpartisan ballot; actual party affiliation Democrat (filed 2022 Democratic primary for FL House District 41; FL Dem Party chair Nikki Fried publicly celebrated her 2025 win; endorsed by Run For Something). Born to a St. Lucian immigrant family, raised in Brooklyn NY; relocated to Central Florida 2000. Education: Poinciana HS; AA General Studies, Valencia College (2007); BA Supervision & Management, Polk State College (2015); MA Public Administration, American Public University (2018). Career: former City of Orlando staff in Economic Development (Downtown Development Board/CRA) and Permitting Office; founder/CEO of Change For The Community (CFTC, est. July 4, 2019); Executive Director of Eatonville CRA. Civic: NAACP, League of Women Voters, American Red Cross, National Congress of Black Women. Single mother; widow (lost husband to gun violence).
Orlando City Commissioner D5 (NP ballot, actual Democrat); sworn June 24, 2024 (special), re-elected Nov 4, 2025; term to 2029. [2026-06 RESOLUTE refinement] HARD evidence (mostly from 2022 HD41 Winter Garden Vox candidate questionnaire + 2024-25 council platform): (a) 2022 questionnaire — 'women should have access to reproductive healthcare and not be forced to give birth to children that might create a financial hardship for them' — sanctity_of_life q1 FALSE; (b) self-described 'survivor of gun violence' (lost husband); platform supports 'reduction of gun violence' and school-safety measures including metal detectors / standardized LE training / secure access points — self_defense q1 FALSE (gun-control orientation; FL 790.33 preempts city ordinances regardless); (c) Fraternal Order of Police endorsement; platform centers 'community policing'; took credit for '27% drop in crime' under her tenure — public_justice q0 TRUE (FOP endorsement + affirmative public backing clears high bar). NULL on biblical marriage, family/school (no city school authority), Christian liberty, economic stewardship (no individual Rose vote against the 12-year flat millage on record either way), election integrity, refuse_state_overreach, and border (Mayor Dyer not Rose handled the Jan 2026 ICE-facility statement). Religion not publicly stated. Active investigation flag (not rubric-scored): March 2026 OPD referred her for FDLE investigation over alleged use of anonymous private email accounts from City Hall network to send harassing messages to a Pulse survivor advocacy group; Regina Hill has also filed a Nov 2025 election-fraud lawsuit against Rose and the canvassing board (Rose called it 'frivolous'). Both pending; flagged as governance-conduct context only.
