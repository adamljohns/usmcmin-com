Official Profile

Education: BA (history), MPA, JD — University of Texas at Austin
Background: Immigration/criminal-defense attorney (represented hundreds in deportation/asylum proceedings); former board chair, Workers Defense Project; won the Jan 2022 D4 special election, re-elected Nov 2024 (58.5%); serving as Mayor Pro Tem in 2026; term ends Jan 2029.
Next Election Year: 2028
Austin City Council D4 (nonpartisan office; Democrat), Mayor Pro Tem 2026; re-elected Nov 2024 with 58.5%, term to Jan 2029. Evidence-finalized 2026-06-10. HARD contrary evidence: authored the GRACE Act (July 2022, passed unanimously) making abortion-related enforcement APD's lowest priority and called the state ban 'draconian'; introduced the May 2024 resolution (passed 10-1) deprioritizing SB 14 enforcement (gender-transition treatments for minors) — first Texas city — saying 'Trans people deserve the right to self determination'; presented the 'Y'all Means All Day' LGBT proclamation; part of the Aug 2025 subquorum proposing a 6.75-cent property-tax increase and joined the 5-cent tax-rate-election motion (voters rejected it Nov 2025; still favors taxing to the 3.5% state cap); champion of marijuana-enforcement deprioritization and praised Travis County personal-bond magistration ('probably the best magistration system in the state'); led the Jan 2026 APD policy restricting ICE cooperation ('communication with ICE should be an exceptional circumstance') — now under a Paxton investigation. Credit where earned (public_justice q0 TRUE): voted YES on the 5-year $218M police contract (Oct 2024, 10-1), publicly calling it a 'major, major win' that 'accomplishes everything the APOA requires,' and publicly praised police/EMS/fire response to the 2026 6th Street mass shooting — affirmative public backing, no defund support documented (joined council after the 2020 budget cut); also advocates police-oversight enforcement and delayed transfer of 37 civilian positions to APD (2023). refuse_state_overreach left null: his repeated resistance to TX state law (abortion, SB 14, marijuana, SB4/ICE) runs in the progressive direction against the rubric's intent. No public religious profession found.
