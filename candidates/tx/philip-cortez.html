Official Profile

Background: TX House District 117 (western San Antonio/Bexar Co.), Democrat; elected 2012, lost 2014, regained 2016, re-elected since. U.S. Air Force Reserve captain; former San Antonio City Council member (2007-2011); PhD in Educational Administration (UT Austin). On Homeland Security/Public Safety/Veterans' Affairs and Intergovernmental Affairs. Considered a business-friendly moderate.
TX State Representative, HD-117 (D), multiple terms since 2012; Air Force Reserve captain. [2026-06 national refinement, state tier] Progressive on the marquee social issues but a documented CROSS-PARTY OUTLIER on several others: voted NO on SB8 (2021 heartbeat), HB1280 (trigger ban), and SB4 (Choice Tracker), is PP Texas Votes-endorsed, voted YES on HB1738 (2023 repeal of the homosexual-conduct crime), voted NO on SB2 (2025 voucher), and opposed SB1 strenuously enough to break quorum in 2021 (though he was the first Democrat to return to negotiate, and declined to break quorum at all in 2025). Conservative groups rate him low (Freedom Index 13%, Texans for Fiscal Responsibility 'F'). DEVIATIONS (broke RIGHT of the D line, per Freedom Index roll-call records): YES on HB796 (Texas Sovereignty Act / 10th-Amendment anti-commandeering), YES on HB9 (2023 border-security funds / Operation Lone Star), and YES on HB1927 (2021 permitless carry); plus YES on HB1056 sound-money and SB1070 (ERIC withdrawal). CAUTION (transparency): the HB1927 vote comes from a single secondary source (Freedom Index) and could not be re-confirmed against the TLO roll call this pass; the HB9 border vote is scored as documented support for active border presence. Texas Alliance for Life rates him 59% (consensus maternal-health bills, not a pro-life shift). Do NOT conflate the Freedom-Index '2023 SB8 - Yes' entry with the 2021 SB8 ban, which he voted NO on.
