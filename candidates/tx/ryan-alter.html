Official Profile

Education: BS Engineering, University of Texas at Austin; JD Harvard Law School
Background: Austin City Council D5 (south-central Austin/Zilker) since Jan 2023. Attorney; former Texas Senate staffer (Watson, Garcia, Hinojosa). Married, three children. Running for re-election Nov 2026. Not related to former D10 member Alison Alter.
Next Election Year: 2026
Evidence-finalized 2026-06-10 (local autopilot, Austin batch-3). Sitting D5 member, web-verified June 2026 (quoted in role May 2026); fundraising for the Nov 2026 ballot. In the 7-vote majority on the Aug 2024 FY2025 abortion-travel-fund amendment (the four no votes were named and he was not among them). Co-sponsored the May 2024 SB 14 trans-protections resolution (first Texas city to deprioritize enforcement). Publicly campaigned for the Prop Q 5-cent tax-rate increase, calling its 63% defeat the loss of a 'lifeline.' On HB 2127 preemption: 'This bill really is a true threat to what cities can do.' Defended Austin's policy limiting ICE cooperation on administrative warrants and signed the 2026 council letter against 'unconstitutional and violent federal operations.' Public-justice cells held to the high bar: his police-contract posture was oversight-conditional (would vote down any contract not implementing the Police Oversight Act) plus a quarterly police-spend transparency resolution — neither affirmative pro-police backing nor defund advocacy → null. Voted yes on HOME zoning density reform (Dec 2023, 9-2). Gun cells null — the red-flag advocacy in Austin coverage belongs to Alison Alter, not him.
