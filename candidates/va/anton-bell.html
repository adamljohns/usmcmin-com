Official Profile

Religion: Ordained minister at Calvary Revival Church (Hampton); serves with wife Dr. Dawn Yvette Bell in youth ministries
Education: Norfolk Catholic High School; B.A. Political Science, Norfolk State University; J.D. (with Honors), North Carolina Central School of Law
Background: Anton A. Bell; first African-American elected Hampton Commonwealth's Attorney. Career career prosecutor — handled violent crimes, homicides, and child molestation in Hampton; served as Special Assistant U.S. Attorney (E.D. Va.) on the Peninsula Drug Task Force. Appointed CA 2012 after retirement of Linda Curtis; won the 2012 special election and was re-elected in 2013, 2017 (over Nicholas Hobbs), 2021 (unopposed), and 2023 (unopposed); current term Jan 1, 2024 – Dec 31, 2027; next election November 2027. Currently serving as Immediate Past President of the Virginia Association of Commonwealth's Attorneys (VACA), 2025–26. Married to Dr. Dawn Yvette Bell.
Serving since 2012 (appointed, then won special election). Running unopposed for 4th term. B.A. Norfolk State, J.D. NC Central. Immediate Past President of VA Assoc. of Commonwealth's Attorneys.
