Official Profile

Religion: Institutionally Baptist — public bio states she previously served on the governing board of St. Peter Baptist Church Child Development Center. No public personal profession of faith in office located. Distinct from her late husband Rep. A. Donald McEachin, who was an ordained AME minister.
Education: B.A. Brown University; J.D. University of Virginia School of Law (per primary bio at rva.gov/commonwealths-attorney/about — note: prior brief speculation 'William & Mary undergrad' is incorrect).
Background: First woman and first African-American woman elected Commonwealth's Attorney for the City of Richmond. 25+ years in the Richmond CA's office before winning the 2019 special general election after predecessor Mike Herring resigned to join McGuireWoods (May 2019). Re-elected unopposed in 2021. Won the June 17 2025 Democratic primary over Tom Barbour Jr. (~71% vs ~29%) and ran unopposed in the Nov 4 2025 general; new term seated Jan 2026. Widow of the late Rep. A. Donald McEachin (VA-04), who died Nov 2022; they met in law school at UVA and have three children. Life member Delta Sigma Theta, NAACP, Richmond Crusade for Voters. Notable: she publicly DECLINED to join 'Virginia Progressive Prosecutors for Justice,' saying she 'doesn't need to join a club' to make change — her 2025 primary challenger Tom Barbour ran from her left as the explicitly-progressive challenger, asserting 'Colette McEachin is not a progressive prosecutor' (Virginia Mercury, VPM).
