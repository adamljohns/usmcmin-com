Virginia Congressional Redistricting Amendment

Statewide — All Virginia Voters

This is a statewide constitutional amendment that would allow the Virginia General Assembly to redraw U.S. congressional district maps — overriding the bipartisan redistricting commission that voters approved in 2020.

What's on the Ballot

The ballot question asks whether the Constitution should be amended to "allow the General Assembly to temporarily adopt new congressional districts to restore fairness in the upcoming elections." Originates from House Joint Resolution 4007 (HJ6007). The legislature's authority would expire after October 31, 2030.

What It Actually Does

  • Strips the bipartisan commission: In 2020, Virginia voters approved a bipartisan redistricting commission. This amendment suspends that commission and hands map-drawing power back to the Democrat-controlled General Assembly.
  • Current map: Republicans hold 5 seats, Democrats hold 6 seats (11 total).
  • Proposed new map: Under maps favored by supporters, Democrats would hold 10 of 11 seats — a gain of 4 seats.
  • Trigger mechanism: Activates if another state redraws its own congressional districts before 2031 without court order.
  • Funding disparity: "Virginians for Fair Elections" (YES) has raised $49 million. "Virginians for Fair Maps" (NO) has raised $9 million.

Who Supports YES

  • Governor Abigail Spanberger (D)
  • Former President Barack Obama
  • U.S. Senators Tim Kaine and Mark Warner (both D)
  • NAACP Virginia
  • Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee

Who Supports NO

  • Former Governor Glenn Youngkin (R)
  • Former Lt. Governor Winsome Earle-Sears (R)
  • U.S. Representatives Ben Cline, Morgan Griffith, Jen Kiggans (R)
  • Republican Party of Virginia
  • Some Democrats who originally fought for the independent commission

Polling

Washington Post/Schar School poll (March 26-31, 2026): Support 52%, Oppose 47% — tight margin. NBC News reported Democrats are "starting to sweat" as the outcome is "not a done deal."

Fredericksburg Voting Info

  • Early voting: March 6 – April 18 at FXBG City Center, 601 Caroline Street, 5th floor (weekdays 8:30 AM – 4:30 PM; Saturdays April 11 & 18, 9 AM – 5 PM)
  • Voter registration deadline: April 14, 2026
  • Absentee ballot request deadline: April 10, 2026
  • Election Day: April 21, 2026

Reformed Christian Analysis

This amendment is a power grab dressed in the language of fairness. Virginia voters in 2020 chose to take redistricting out of partisan hands. This amendment reverses that decision and concentrates map-drawing power in a Democrat-controlled legislature that has voted to expand abortion, ban firearms, promote gender ideology in schools, and suppress parental rights. The proposed maps would eliminate conservative representation in 4 congressional districts. Christians who value the election of godly magistrates should understand what a YES vote means: fewer representatives who share their values in Congress.

A rally was held in Fredericksburg in late March 2026 where speakers urged voters to reject this amendment. "Vote No April 21" signs lined the roadway.

2025 Virginia Statewide Elections

Commonwealth of Virginia

Virginia holds gubernatorial elections in odd years. In 2025, voters will elect a new Governor, Lieutenant Governor, and Attorney General, along with all 100 House of Delegates members.

What's at Stake

  • Governor: Controls executive branch, appoints judges and agency heads, sets enforcement priorities
  • Lieutenant Governor: Presides over State Senate, breaks tie votes
  • Attorney General: Chief law enforcement officer, sets legal policy, can challenge federal overreach
  • 100 House Delegates: All 100 seats up for election. Currently Democrat-controlled. Determines legislation on abortion, guns, education, and religious liberty

Christian Voter Priority

  • Will the next Governor sign or veto abortion restrictions?
  • Will the AG defend or attack religious liberty cases?
  • Will the House maintain or expand gun control measures passed in recent sessions?
  • Will parental rights in education be strengthened or weakened?

Reformed Christian Analysis

Virginia's 2025 election is the most consequential state election in the nation. The Governor, AG, and House of Delegates control whether Virginia moves toward or away from Christian principles in law. Use the RESOLUTE Citizen Scorecard to evaluate every candidate before you vote.

School Board Watch: Spotsylvania & Stafford

Spotsylvania County & Stafford County, Virginia

School boards control curriculum, library materials, bathroom policies, and the moral environment your children spend 7+ hours in daily. Spotsylvania County's school board made national headlines for its conservative stance on removing sexually explicit materials from school libraries.

Key Issues

  • Sexually Explicit Materials: Are graphic books available in school libraries? Who decides what's appropriate?
  • Gender Ideology: Are schools using preferred pronouns, allowing opposite-sex bathroom access, or teaching gender theory?
  • Parental Notification: Are parents informed when their child expresses gender confusion at school?
  • Critical Race Theory: Is CRT or DEI ideology embedded in curriculum or teacher training?
  • Bible/Prayer: Is voluntary religious expression protected or suppressed?

Reformed Christian Analysis

Parents, not the state, are the primary educators of their children under God's design (Deuteronomy 6:6-7). School boards that undermine parental authority or expose children to sexually explicit or ideologically corrupt material are acting contrary to biblical principles. Attend school board meetings. Know your board members. Hold them accountable.
