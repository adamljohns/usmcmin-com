What this measures

Does this candidate defend the unalienable right to bear arms as a check against tyranny and a defense of household, neighbor, and homeland?

Why it matters

The Second Amendment recognizes — it does not grant — a right that predates the Constitution. The God-given duty of every head-of-household to defend his family (1 Timothy 5:8) requires the practical means to do so. A disarmed Christian population is a population without recourse when civil authorities turn unjust, and history (Russia 1917, Germany 1938, China 1949, Cambodia 1975, Venezuela 2012) is unambiguous about what happens next. This category catches the politicians who voted for the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act, who took the NRA grade then voted with Bloomberg, who quietly funded the ATF's pistol-brace reclassification.

Key bills, votes, and laws we track

  • Constitutional Carry / Permitless Carry Acts — State-by-state — 29 states as of 2025. Adam tracks every governor signature + state-rep vote.
  • National Reciprocity Act (HR 38) — Federal recognition of state concealed-carry permits across state lines.
  • Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (2022) — Yes-vote = -2 in this category. 15 Republican senators crossed.
  • Repeal the NFA / Hearing Protection Act — Co-sponsorship gets full credit.

Organizations and PAC vectors we score

  • Gun Owners of America (GOA) — Strictest pro-2A grading. A-rating = positive signal.
  • National Rifle Association (NRA) — Useful but inflated — verify NRA grades against actual votes.
  • Virginia Citizens Defense League (VCDL) — State-level Virginia tracking.
  • Everytown for Gun Safety / Moms Demand Action — Endorsements disqualify.
  • Brady Campaign — Endorsements disqualify.

⚠️ Position-level disqualifiers

  • Voted YES on the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act (2022)
  • Voted YES on a red-flag law or "extreme risk protection order" statute
  • Took identifiable Bloomberg / Everytown / Brady PAC money

A candidate matching any of the above is graded down to the floor of this category regardless of other answers in the rubric.
