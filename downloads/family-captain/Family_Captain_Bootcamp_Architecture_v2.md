## Mission

Equip Christian husbands, fathers, and leaders to use AI — Claude and Cowork primary, supported by ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Whisper, and note-taking tools — as disciplined adjutants for life at home, in business, and in ministry. Christ at the center, the captain in command, AI on the bridge as a trusted aide. Not autopilots. Not toys.

## Operating Principles (the spine)

1. **Christ first, tools second.** Every week opens and closes in Scripture. AI is stewardship, not idolatry.
2. **Tiered autonomy.** Every workflow gets classified — *informational*, *contextual*, or *sensitive* — so men learn what to delegate and what to keep on the bridge.
3. **Drafting vs. execution.** AI drafts. The captain decides. We separate these on day one and never blur them.
4. **Operator mindset.** Each task ships a working artifact (a prompt, a routine, a doc, a script). No theory without application.
5. **No hype.** No model worship. Reliability, ROI, and durability over novelty.
6. **Imperfect actions.** Borrowed from the community vocabulary. Done beats perfect. Captain's actions stack.
7. **Multiplication.** Week 5 is built so each captain can teach the next one (2 Timothy 2:2).

## Format & Rhythm

- **Duration:** 5 weeks
- **Cadence:** 3 Tasks per week, dropped Mon / Wed / Fri (or any rhythm the cohort host picks)
- **Time per task:** 20–30 minutes
- **Optional Captain's Drill:** one 90-second prompt per day, 35 in total (depth-seekers only)
- **Cohort touch points:** Monday Kickoff post, Wednesday Captain's Call, Friday Close-out — that's the **heartbeat post** rhythm
- **Sabbath posture:** Sundays are off. Heartbeat posts pause. Captain's Drills pause.

## Voice & Lexicon (locked)

- Brotherly captain-to-captain. Peer, not preacher.
- Direct, simple, second person. ("Make it count.")
- Gentle where it matters. Permission for dry humor.
- Spiritual frame woven in casually, not stacked.
- Use the community's existing terms naturally: **Captain**, **Captain's actions**, **Captain's call**, **Captain's log**, **Captain's charter**, **SACA list**, **FPM**, **imperfect actions**, **Bootcamp**.
- Comments are part of the design. Every post invites engagement.

## Week 1 — Foundations: The Captain's Mindset

> *Why a man uses AI matters more than which model he picks.*

### Task 1: Write Your AI Charter
- Open Claude or ChatGPT (whichever you already have).
- Write 5 lines. What is AI for in your life? What is it not for?
- Save it. This is your first guardrail.
- Post one line of it in the comments. Iron sharpens iron.

### Task 2: Draw the Line — Stewardship vs. Dependence
- Map 3 places AI could quietly replace prayer, Scripture, or wise counsel.
- For each, write your guardrail in one sentence ("I will not _____ with AI.")
- Hard rule: AI never replaces the Spirit, the Word, or your wife's voice.

### Task 3: Build Your One-Page Tool Stack
- One page. Five tools max: Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, Whisper.
- For each, write one line: *what I use it for.* If you don't have a use, leave it blank — that's honest.
- This becomes your "captain's compass." You'll update it Week 5.

**Scripture anchor for the week:** Proverbs 3:5–6 — leaning not on our own understanding (or our tools).

## Week 2 — Personal Operating System

> *The captain has a daily rhythm. AI runs alongside it, not over it.*

### Task 1: Build a 5-Minute Morning Anchor
- Build one prompt template you'll use every morning to plan your day.
- Inputs: today's calendar, top 3 priorities, one verse you're sitting in.
- Output: a 5-line plan you can read in 30 seconds.
- Use it tomorrow. Adjust by Friday.

### Task 2: Capture with Voice — Whisper Workflow
- Open Voice Memos (or Whisper directly). Record 3 minutes — a thought, a sermon idea, a list of fears.
- Run it through Whisper or your Apple transcript.
- Paste it into Claude. Ask: "Pull the 3 things I should act on this week."
- Send one of those three to your wife or a brother.

### Task 3: Tag Your Tasks — Tiered Autonomy in Practice
- List 10 of your recurring tasks (work, family, ministry).
- Tag each one: **informational** (AI can answer), **contextual** (AI can draft, you decide), **sensitive** (AI stays out).
- This is the most important framework in the whole bootcamp. Don't skim it.

**Scripture anchor for the week:** Psalm 90:12 — teach us to number our days.

## Week 3 — Husband, Father, Brother: At Home

> *Tools serve the home. They never compete with it.*

### Task 1: Prep One Hard Conversation (Don't Hide Behind AI)
- There's a conversation you've been avoiding with your wife or a kid.
- Use Claude to *prepare* it: clarify what you want to say, anticipate her response, find the kindest true words.
- **Then have the conversation. Without AI in the room.**
- AI prepares the captain. The captain shows up.

### Task 2: Build a Family Rhythm Asset
- Pick one: a 2-week meal plan + grocery list, a chore rotation, a family devotion track, or a kids' summer reading list.
- Generate it with Cowork. Print it. Stick it on the fridge.
- Bonus: ask your wife what *she* would automate. Build that next.

### Task 3: Write Your Family AI Policy (Together)
- Sit down with your wife. 15 minutes.
- Write 5 rules together: when AI is on, when it's off, what kids see, what stays adults-only, what's never asked of AI in this house.
- Sign it. Post it on the fridge or in your shared note.
- This is Joshua 24:15 in 2026 form.

**Scripture anchor for the week:** Joshua 24:15 — as for me and my house.

## Week 4 — Business, Ministry & Community

> *Cowork mode is where leverage lives. Use it carefully.*

### Task 1: Buy Back 5 Hours This Month
- List 5 hours/week you're spending on tasks AI can draft.
- Pick ONE. Build a Cowork workflow for it this week.
- Constraint: it **drafts only** — you press send. Drafting vs. execution stays separate.
- Post in the comments: which hour did you buy back?

### Task 2: Build One Ministry Workflow
- Sermon prep, Bible study, outreach email, follow-up on a visitor — pick one.
- Build a repeatable Claude workflow you'd use again next Sunday.
- Test it on a real task this week, not a fake one.
- Watch for AI fingerprint phrases ("delve," "elevate," em-dashes everywhere). Strip them. Sound like *you.*

### Task 3: Save Your 3 Best Prompts as Skills
- The captain doesn't rewrite prompts every Monday.
- Pick the 3 prompts you actually use. Save them as named skills (Claude Skills, ChatGPT GPTs, or just a `prompts.md` file).
- One of them gets posted in the comments. We learn from each other's captain's logs.

**Scripture anchor for the week:** Colossians 3:23 — whatever you do, work as for the Lord.

## Week 5 — Mastery, Stewardship & Multiplication

> *Build durable systems. Train the next captain.*

### Task 1: Run a Cost & Trap Audit
- Open your AI subscriptions and API bills. Add them up. (Be honest with yourself and your wife.)
- Pick ONE optimization: cancel a tool you don't use, switch a task to a cheaper model, or move a heavy workflow to API pricing.
- Then run 3 trap tests: ask your favorite model something you *know* is wrong. See if it sycophantically agrees. Note where it failed.

### Task 2: Teach One Person, This Week
- Pick someone — your wife, an older kid, a brother, a pastor, a mentee.
- Teach them ONE thing you learned in the last 5 weeks. Not all of it. One thing.
- Make them do it once with you watching. That's how multiplication actually happens.

### Task 3: Write Your Captain's Charter
- One page. Three sections:
  - **My posture** — how I will (and will not) use AI as a man under God.
  - **My systems** — the 3 workflows I will run weekly going forward.
  - **My multiplication plan** — who I will teach next, and by when.
- Sign it. Date it. Post a screenshot in the bootcamp post.
- This is your commissioning.

**Scripture anchor for the week:** 2 Timothy 2:2 — entrust to faithful men who will teach others.

## Captain's Drills — 35 Optional Daily Micro-Prompts

For depth-seekers. 90 seconds each. Skip if your week is already full. The drill is the rep, not the workout.

### Week 1 (Foundations)
1. Ask Claude: *"What's the one assumption I'm making about AI that's probably wrong?"*
2. Rewrite your worst prompt of last week — the one you're embarrassed about.
3. Read Proverbs 3:5–6 aloud. Then ask Claude what *it* would do with that verse. Notice the difference.
4. Open three models with the same question. Compare. Pick the one that earned its keep.
5. Time yourself: how long does it take you to get a useful answer? Cut it in half tomorrow.
6. Ask AI to roast your tool stack. Take the criticism that lands; ignore the rest.
7. *Sabbath. Drill off.*

### Week 2 (Personal OS)
8. Run your morning anchor prompt. Adjust one input.
9. Voice-memo your evening review. Whisper it. Read it tomorrow.
10. Triage one folder of email with Cowork. Draft only — you send.
11. Block one calendar gap and label it "Captain's bridge time."
12. Write one note in your second-brain inbox. Just one. Habit beats volume.
13. Re-tag one task that you mis-classified (sensitive that you put in informational, or vice versa).
14. *Sabbath. Drill off.*

### Week 3 (At Home)
15. Send your wife one sentence of appreciation, drafted by you, polished by AI.
16. Ask Claude to give you 3 questions to ask your kid at dinner. Use one.
17. Do a 10-minute Scripture study with Claude as a study partner — you lead, it serves.
18. Pick a household chore. Automate the *planning* (not the doing).
19. Read your Family AI Policy aloud. Adjust one rule.
20. Tell your wife one thing you're learning in the bootcamp. Listen to her response.
21. *Sabbath. Drill off.*

### Week 4 (Business / Ministry)
22. Draft one customer/community email using your saved prompt. Send it yourself.
23. Run your sermon-prep workflow on a passage you're not preaching. See if it holds.
24. List one task you'll *never* delegate to AI, and why.
25. Strip three AI-fingerprint phrases from something you wrote yesterday.
26. Ask Cowork to summarize your last 7 days in one paragraph. See what it missed.
27. Save one new prompt as a skill. Name it like a captain — clear, terse, useful.
28. *Sabbath. Drill off.*

### Week 5 (Mastery & Multiplication)
29. Audit one subscription. Decide: keep, kill, downgrade.
30. Run one workflow you built earlier. Has it drifted? Fix it.
31. Find one logging gap in your system. Close it.
32. Ask AI to argue *against* your charter. Strengthen the weak points.
33. Teach one new person one thing. Watch them do it once.
34. Write the first sentence of your Captain's Charter. Just the first.
35. Sign your charter. Post the screenshot.

## 90-Day Continuation Rhythm (after Day 35)

Solves the cliff:
- **Day 45 + Day 60:** two heartbeat posts — short, captain-to-captain, "what shipped, what slipped"
- **Day 66–90:** one 60-minute live captain's call — accountability, war stories, one new tactic
- **Quarterly thereafter:** one captain's call per quarter, optional rotating teaching slot for graduates
- **Always-on:** a "captain's log" prompt graduates can reuse weekly

These ship alongside Phase 1 (small surface, high retention).

## What Stays Out (intentionally)

- No model benchmark debates. Captains use what works.
- No deep agentic engineering. This is leadership formation, not an SDK course. Cowork-level patterns only.
- No platform-specific deep dives (e.g., a full Notion workshop). Tools serve the man; the man doesn't serve the platform.
- No politics. AI policy is real, but off-mission for this cohort.
- No prosperity-gospel framing of productivity. Time is stewardship, not status.
