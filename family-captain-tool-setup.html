FAMILY CAPTAIN · TOOL SETUP

Set up your tools.

Plain, step-by-step walkthroughs for the tools this bootcamp leans on. Set one up, run the first captain move, then move on — you don’t need all of them at once.

← Back to the Captain’s Compass
Claude · ChatGPT · Perplexity · Apple Voice Memos · Whisper (voice → text) · NotebookLM · Anthropic Cowork · Viktor · Midjourney · Zapier

Most captains need two or three of these, not all ten. Start with Claude and a way to capture your voice; add the rest as a week calls for them. Prices and screens reflect 2026 and change often — the steps are the shape, not the gospel. We’ll add more tools here over time.

Claude

Free tier · Pro $20/mo · Max from $100/mo

Your primary AI — writing, thinking, planning, and (in Cowork mode) doing multi-step work on your computer. If you set up one tool, make it this one.

Open claude.ai →
Set it up
  1. Go to claude.ai and create a free account (email or Google sign-in).
  2. On your phone, install the Claude app and sign in with the same account.
  3. Start a chat. Tell it who you are and what you’re working on — context makes every answer sharper.
  4. When the free limits start pinching and you’re using it daily, upgrade to Pro ($20).
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Paste your rough Captain’s Charter and ask: ‘Where is this vague? What would a wiser man add?’

ChatGPT

Free tier · Plus $20/mo

The other big general assistant — strong image generation (GPT Image 2) and a hands-off Agent for computer tasks. A solid #2 and sanity-check next to Claude.

Open chatgpt.com →
Set it up
  1. Go to chatgpt.com and sign up free.
  2. Install the mobile app; try voice mode if you’d rather talk it through.
  3. Generate an image by typing ‘make an image of …’ right in the chat.
  4. Plus ($20) unlocks the better models, image gen, and ChatGPT Agent.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Ask it to generate a simple one-page weekly family rhythm as an image you can print for the fridge.

Perplexity

Free tier covers most captains · Pro $20/mo

Search-first AI that answers with real, cited sources — your replacement for ten open Google tabs.

Open perplexity.ai →
Set it up
  1. Go to perplexity.ai (no account needed to try; sign up to save threads).
  2. Ask a real question — it answers and shows the sources it used.
  3. Click a source to verify before you trust it. Always verify.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Research one decision you’re weighing this month: ask for the strongest case on each side, with sources.

Apple Voice Memos

Free (iOS 18+ on a recent iPhone)

The recorder already on your iPhone. On iOS 18+ it transcribes your voice to text on-device — the simplest ‘talk instead of type’ on-ramp.

About Apple Intelligence →
Set it up
  1. Open Voice Memos (already installed). Tap the red button, talk, tap stop.
  2. Tap the recording, then the transcript icon, to read it as text.
  3. Use Share to copy the transcript into Claude or ChatGPT to clean up or summarize.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE On your next drive, voice-memo your thoughts on one hard decision; tonight, paste the transcript into Claude and ask it to organize them.

Whisper (voice → text)

Free (Voice Memos / local) · MacWhisper: free tier + one-time upgrade

Whisper is the open-source engine behind most great transcription — you don’t install it from a code repo, you use an app built on it. On iPhone, Apple Voice Memos already is Whisper-class. On a Mac, MacWhisper is the friendly way to transcribe long files.

Get MacWhisper (Mac) →
Set it up
  1. On iPhone: just use Apple Voice Memos (above) — same quality, zero setup.
  2. On a Mac, for interviews or long recordings: install MacWhisper, drag in an audio file, get a clean transcript.
  3. Paste the transcript into Claude and ask it to summarize or pull action items.

The raw open-source model lives at github.com/openai/whisper — that’s for developers. Most captains never need to touch it; use Voice Memos or MacWhisper instead.

YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Record a 5-minute talk or sermon note, transcribe it, and ask Claude: ‘Pull the 3 action items for me.’

NotebookLM

Free (Google account)

Google’s free research notebook. Drop in PDFs, docs, even your bootcamp materials; ask questions across them; generate a shockingly-natural audio overview for your commute.

Open notebooklm.google.com →
Set it up
  1. Go to notebooklm.google.com and sign in with a Google account.
  2. Create a notebook, click Add, and upload PDFs/docs or paste links.
  3. Ask questions in the chat — answers cite the exact source page.
  4. Click ‘Audio Overview’ to generate a podcast-style summary.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Upload this week’s task PDFs and ask: ‘What is the captain’s one job this week, in plain words?’

Anthropic Cowork

Included with Claude paid plans (Pro / Max)

Claude that doesn’t just answer — it does. Cowork runs multi-step work on your desktop or phone (research, drafting, organizing files) with you approving each step.

About Cowork →
Set it up
  1. Make sure you have a Claude account on a paid plan (Pro or Max) for full Cowork.
  2. Open Cowork from Claude and describe the outcome you want — not the steps.
  3. Watch it work and approve actions. Start small to build trust before you hand it bigger jobs.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Ask Cowork to turn your messy notes file into a clean one-page weekly plan.

Viktor

Paid team plans · free trial — pricing at viktor.com

An AI ‘coworker’ that lives in Slack or Microsoft Teams and actually executes work across your business tools — reports, CRM updates, recurring automations. Built for a team, not a solo desktop.

Open viktor.com →
Set it up
  1. Go to viktor.com and start a trial — you’ll connect it to Slack or Teams.
  2. Connect the business tools your team already uses.
  3. Ask Viktor to do a real recurring job — e.g. a Monday status summary — and let it run.
  4. Solo family captain? Try Claude’s Cowork or ChatGPT Agent first; Viktor shines for teams.

Added after John Michael Clark started using it — pending Adam’s hands-on review of fit and pricing.

YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE If your ministry or business runs in Slack/Teams, have Viktor draft your weekly status update from the channel.

Midjourney

Basic $10/mo · Standard $30/mo

The best-looking AI image generator — sermon graphics, social posts, blog images, flyers, anything that needs to look good.

Open midjourney.com →
Set it up
  1. Go to midjourney.com and sign in (Google or Discord).
  2. Use the web ‘Create’ box; type a clear description (subject, style, mood).
  3. Refine with variations and upscales until one’s right, then download it.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Generate a clean banner image for your family’s verse of the month.

Zapier

Free tier · paid plans scale with volume

The wiring between your apps: ‘when X happens, do Y’ — no code. Add an AI step to summarize, draft, or sort automatically.

Open zapier.com →
Set it up
  1. Sign up at zapier.com (free).
  2. Create a Zap: pick a trigger (e.g. a new email labeled ‘Giving’) and an action (add a row to a sheet).
  3. Add an AI step if you want it to summarize or categorize along the way.
  4. Turn it on; it runs quietly in the background.
YOUR FIRST CAPTAIN MOVE Automate one nagging weekly copy-paste chore — let it run while you’re with your family.
