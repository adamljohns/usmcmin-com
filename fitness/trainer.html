My Story

I'm Adam Johns — most people call me Moop. After 20 years of military service in the United States Marine Corps, I walked out of uniform with a conviction: discipline is a gift, not a punishment. The Corps taught me what the human body is capable of under pressure, under load, under fire. That never left me.

I became a NASM-certified trainer because I wanted to bring that same standard to everyday people — not elite athletes, not gym rats — regular people who want to feel strong, live long, and show up fully for their families. I specialize in body composition, visceral fat reduction, and the behavioral side of sustainable change.

At fit20 Cosner's Corner, I've seen what 20 minutes of properly programmed slow-rep resistance training does to people who thought they'd never have time to train. It changes their posture. Their confidence. Their relationship with their own body. That's the work I was made for.

Coaching Philosophy

Health isn't vanity. It's stewardship. The Fit Shepherd framework isn't about aesthetics. It's about building the kind of physical discipline that makes you steady, strong, and unshakeable for the long haul.

Discipline First

Systems over willpower. Build habits that outlast motivation.

Efficient Training

20 minutes of slow-rep HIT beats 60 minutes of busy work.

Protein-Led Nutrition

Macro strategy anchored to lean muscle and visceral fat reduction.

Faith-Integrated

Physical discipline as an act of worship. Stewardship, not performance.
