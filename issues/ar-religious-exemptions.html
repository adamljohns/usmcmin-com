Arkansas: Religious Exemptions for Nondiscrimination

State of Arkansas

The Arkansas legislature passed two significant religious exemption bills in 2025. HB 1616 creates religious exemptions for nondiscrimination protections, and HB 0207 allows religious exemptions that affect housing, healthcare, and employment decisions.

These laws provide important protections for people of faith who object to participating in activities that violate their sincerely held religious beliefs, particularly regarding sexual orientation and gender identity issues.

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

Religious liberty protections are essential in a time when people of faith face increasing pressure to compromise their beliefs. These exemptions protect the right of Christians to live according to their convictions.
