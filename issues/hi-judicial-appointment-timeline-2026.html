Hawaii Senate Judicial-Appointment Review Window Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Hawaii Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Extends the time the Hawaii Senate has to confirm or reject a judicial appointment from 30 to 60 days.
  • The longer window applies only to appointments made April 1 through December 31, the stretch when the Senate is frequently not in session.
  • Sponsors say it lets the Senate vet nominees without convening a costly special session just to beat the 30-day clock.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Senate Bill 121 (2025) was referred to voters by the Legislature.
  • Reported chamber votes: Senate 25-0 (March 4, 2025) and House 42-6 (April 4, 2025).
  • A simple statewide majority is required to ratify it on the November 3, 2026 ballot.

Who Supports This

  • Sen. Karl Rhoads (D-13), sponsor
  • Near-unanimous majorities in both chambers (SB 121)

Who Opposes This

  • No organized opposition; the Hawaii Judiciary raised a neutral concern about prolonged vacancies

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

A judge should be examined before he is confirmed, not waved through against a clock, and giving the Senate a real chance to search out a nominee's record is the cautious, defensible side of this. Folly is answering a matter before you have heard it. The honest counter a careful voter should hold is that a longer review window can also mean longer judicial vacancies and more leverage for the Senate to stall a nominee it simply dislikes. This is housekeeping, not a culture-war fight — it passed with almost no opposition — so weigh it on the merits: do you want appointments vetted more slowly and carefully, or filled faster? On balance it leans 'yes' for anyone who values deliberation over speed on the bench.

"He that answereth a matter before he heareth it, it is folly and shame unto him."

— Proverbs 18:13 (KJV)
