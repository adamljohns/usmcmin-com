Maryland Commission on Judicial Disabilities Vacancies Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Maryland Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Allows the chair of the Commission on Judicial Disabilities to appoint former Commission members to temporarily fill vacancies so the body can keep functioning.
  • Lets the governor appoint a substitute member, or extend a sitting member's term, when a member is recused, disqualified, or has reached term's end while a matter is pending.
  • The 2026 revision adds a guardrail: a temporary substitute previously rejected by the Senate may not serve.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment (60% supermajority of each chamber required).
  • Carried in 2026 as SB 933; it replaced an earlier version (HB 788) that had been referred in the 2025 session. Reported to have passed both chambers; the precise roll call is not independently confirmed here.
  • A simple statewide majority is required to ratify it on the November 3, 2026 ballot.

Who Supports This

  • Maryland General Assembly (reported to have passed both chambers)

Who Opposes This

  • No organized opposition identified

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is administrative plumbing for the body that disciplines Maryland's judges, not a culture-war flashpoint — and that is exactly why a citizen should read it plainly rather than reflexively. A commission that polices judicial misconduct only works if it can keep a quorum; allowing former members to step in and barring Senate-rejected substitutes are reasonable continuity fixes. The one thing worth watching is the expansion of the governor's hand in staffing a body meant to hold the judiciary accountable: any time an executive gains influence over the watchmen of another branch, the separation God built into good government is at stake. On its face this measure is modest and defensible; vote it on the merits of keeping judicial accountability functioning, not on party reflex.
