Michigan Constitutional Convention Question (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Michigan Voters

What The Question Asks

  • Ballot language: whether a convention of elected delegates should be called for the purpose of a general revision of the Michigan Constitution.
  • If voters approve it, a convention would convene in Lansing (reported date October 5, 2027) and any resulting revision would itself have to be submitted back to the voters for ratification.
  • It is a yes-or-no question about reopening the whole charter, not a vote on any specific change.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • This is an automatic referral, not a citizen initiative or a legislative referral. Article XII, Section 3 of the 1963 Michigan Constitution requires the convention question be put to voters in 1978 and every 16 years after — it last appeared in 2010 and would next appear in 2042 if rejected now.
  • No petition signatures or legislative vote were required to place it on the ballot.
  • Michigan voters rejected the same question in 1978, 1994, and 2010.

Who Supports This

  • Michigan House Speaker Matt Hall (R-Richland Twp.)
  • Senate Minority Leader Aric Nesbitt (R-Porter Twp.)

Who Opposes This

  • 'Protect MI Constitution from Special Interests' (registered ballot-question committee opposing the measure)

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

A constitutional convention does not amend one clause — it reopens the entire charter at once, and everything from property rights to the structure of the courts is back on the table. That is a far heavier decision than any single amendment, because the citizen cannot know in advance what a convention of delegates will produce; he can only judge whether the present constitution is broken badly enough to risk a wholesale rewrite. Scripture warns against casually moving the boundary stones the fathers set. The prudent question is not whether the 1963 constitution is perfect, but whether the people trust the delegates and special interests who would dominate a convention to hand back something better, and whether ordinary families would gain or lose protections in the trade. Demand specifics before voting yes on a blank check.

"Remove not the ancient landmark, which thy fathers have set."

— Proverbs 22:28 (KJV)
