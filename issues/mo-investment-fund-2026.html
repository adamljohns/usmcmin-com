Missouri Show-Me Prosperity Investment Fund Amendment (SJR 95, Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Missouri Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Establishes a state-owned investment fund (described as the 'Show-Me Prosperity' fund) that would invest public dollars in S&P 500 exchange-traded funds.
  • Caps annual withdrawals from the fund at 3% of its value.
  • Stated long-term goal is to grow a return stream large enough to eventually reduce or replace tax revenue the state now collects.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in Senate Joint Resolution 95; sponsor Sen. Adam Schnelting (R, St. Charles).
  • Reported votes: Senate 22-7 (March 23, 2026); House 85-56 (May 15, 2026).
  • Ballotpedia News reported the General Assembly placed two constitutional amendments on the November 3, 2026 ballot in this batch (this fund and the county-sheriff amendment).

Who Supports This

  • Sen. Adam Schnelting (R, St. Charles), sponsor
  • The legislative majorities that referred it (Senate 22-7, House 85-56)

Who Opposes This

  • The minority that voted no (7 senators and 56 representatives)

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

A state that puts public money into the market is making a wager with other people's taxes. The instinct behind a sovereign-style fund is sound stewardship — wealth gathered patiently grows, and a 3% withdrawal cap is a real guardrail against a legislature raiding the principal in a lean year. But the same fund exposes the public treasury to market downturns, and 'we will eventually replace your taxes' is the kind of promise that outlives the politicians who make it. The wise man keeps treasure and oil in his dwelling; the fool spends it all. The citizen should weigh whether Missouri has the discipline to leave the fund alone in good years and bad, or whether a market fund simply becomes a new pot for the connected to reach into.

"There is treasure to be desired and oil in the dwelling of the wise; but a foolish man spendeth it up."

— Proverbs 21:20 (KJV)
