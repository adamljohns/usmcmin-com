North Carolina Lower Maximum Income Tax Rate Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All North Carolina Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Lowers the constitutional ceiling on the state personal income tax rate from 7 percent to 3.5 percent.
  • The current flat income tax rate is 3.99 percent, so the new cap would sit just below today's rate, tightly constraining future increases.
  • Bars the legislature from raising the income tax above 3.5 percent without amending the constitution again.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Referred via Senate Bill 1080 (2026). North Carolina has no citizen initiative; amendments require a three-fifths vote of each chamber.
  • Reported votes: Senate 30-18; House 73-46.

Who Supports This

  • The legislative majorities that referred it (Senate 30-18, House 73-46)
  • Taxpayer and limited-government advocates

Who Opposes This

  • The 18 senators and 46 representatives who voted no
  • Those who object to locking a tax ceiling into the constitution

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is a tax-limitation question with real teeth. Locking a 3.5 percent ceiling into the constitution makes future income-tax hikes much harder — a durable restraint that favors taxpayers and predictable budgeting. The flip side is the mirror image of the same fact: a constitutional cap ties the hands of future legislatures and voters, including in a downturn. For citizens who prize limited government and guarding the household against tax creep, this points clearly yes. Those who want fiscal flexibility kept in elected hands will weigh it differently. The largely party-line votes reflect that divide.
