North Dakota 60% Vote Requirement for Constitutional Amendments Measure (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All North Dakota Voters

What The Measure Does

  • Raises the threshold to adopt any constitutional amendment from a simple majority (50%+1) to a 60 percent supermajority.
  • Applies to both citizen-initiated and legislature-referred amendments.
  • Makes North Dakota's constitution harder to change at the ballot box.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Referred via House Concurrent Resolution 3003 (2025 session).
  • Reported votes: House 65-28 (Jan 29, 2025); Senate 33-13 (Apr 9, 2025).

Who Supports This

  • The legislative majorities that referred it (House 65-28, Senate 33-13)
  • Those who want the state constitution harder to amend

Who Opposes This

  • The 28 representatives and 13 senators who voted no
  • Citizen-initiative advocates who say it weakens direct democracy

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is a question about how easily the state's foundational law can be rewritten. Supporters argue a 60 percent bar protects the constitution from being amended by narrow, well-funded campaigns and keeps it stable; critics counter that it dilutes majority rule and makes citizen initiatives — a check on the legislature — much harder to win. There is a genuine conservative case on both sides: reverence for a stable charter versus the people's direct power to amend it. Note the self-interested angle — the legislature is asking voters to make the initiative process, which can bypass the legislature, harder to use. Weigh whether you value constitutional stability or an accessible citizen check more.
