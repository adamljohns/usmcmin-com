New Mexico Library Acquisition Bond (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All New Mexico Voters

What The Bond Does

  • Authorizes roughly $20 million in general-obligation bonds for acquisitions at public, school, academic, and tribal libraries.
  • Repaid over time through a statewide property tax.
  • Smallest of three bond questions referred together by HB 248 (about $280 million total).

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Referred by HB 248 (2026 session); House 65-0, Senate 38-0; signed March 10, 2026.

Who Supports This

  • The legislature, which referred it unanimously (House 65-0, Senate 38-0)
  • Public, school, and tribal libraries

Who Opposes This

  • Fiscal conservatives wary of new property-tax-backed general-obligation debt

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

The smallest of the three bonds, $20 million for library materials across the state. The stewardship questions are identical to its companion measures: a real but borrowed sum, repaid through property taxes. Libraries are a long-standing public good, and the figure is modest. As with the other bonds, the discipline is to weigh the concrete benefit against the debt, and to remember that approving all three is approving the full $280 million package. Decide each on its merits and on your tolerance for property-tax-backed borrowing.
