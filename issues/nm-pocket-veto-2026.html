New Mexico Eliminate Governor's Pocket Veto and Require Veto Explanations Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All New Mexico Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Ends the pocket veto: a bill the governor neither signs nor returns within the constitutional window becomes law instead of dying.
  • Requires the governor to give the legislature a substantive written reason for any veto.
  • Would make New Mexico the 38th state without a pocket veto.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in HJR 2 (2025 session). New Mexico has no citizen-initiative process; constitutional amendments reach the ballot only by legislative referral.
  • Reported votes: House 66-0 (Feb 27, 2025); Senate 38-0 (Mar 13, 2025) — unanimous in both chambers.
  • Source note: one advocacy tracker labels the resolution “SJR 4”; Ballotpedia reports it as HJR 2. The substance and unanimous votes are consistent across sources.

Who Supports This

  • The bipartisan legislative supermajorities that referred it (House 66-0, Senate 38-0)

Who Opposes This

  • No organized opposition recorded; the measure passed both chambers unanimously

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is a check-and-balance question more than a partisan one — the unanimous votes show it. A pocket veto lets an executive kill a bill by simply sitting on it, with no public reason given; ending it forces the decision into the open and makes the governor own a veto in writing. For a citizen who values government that answers for what it does, that transparency cuts in a healthy direction. The practical stakes are modest — it changes process, not policy — but keeping the lines of accountability clean is itself a public good.
