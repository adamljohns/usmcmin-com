New Mexico Allow School Elections on Partisan Election Dates Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All New Mexico Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Repeals the constitutional rule that school-board and school elections be held on their own separate dates.
  • Lets those elections be consolidated onto regular state or federal partisan election dates.
  • Aim is higher turnout and lower cost by folding off-cycle school elections into the general election.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in SJR 1 (2026 session).
  • Reported votes: Senate 38-0; House 50-9.

Who Supports This

  • The legislators who referred it (Senate 38-0, House 50-9)
  • Turnout and cost-saving advocates

Who Opposes This

  • The nine representatives who voted no
  • Those who prefer school elections kept separate from partisan contests

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

Off-cycle school elections are notorious for tiny turnout — a few percent of voters decide who runs the schools and how the bond money is spent. Folding them into the November general would put those choices in front of far more citizens, which generally favors broader accountability. The tradeoff is that school races would now share a ballot with partisan contests, and critics worry that nationalizes what were nonpartisan local school questions. On balance this is a turnout question with election-integrity overtones; if you think more eyes on school governance is healthy, it points yes.
