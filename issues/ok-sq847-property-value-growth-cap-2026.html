Oklahoma State Question 847 — Lower the Annual Cap on Taxable Property Value Growth Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Oklahoma Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Reduces the annual growth cap on the taxable fair cash value of general property from 5% to 4%.
  • Reduces the annual growth cap for homestead-exempt property from 3% to 1.75%.
  • Repeals the existing prohibition on any increase in fair cash value for the homestead of those 65 and older whose income exceeds the HUD-determined median.
  • Takes effect for tax year 2027.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in Senate Joint Resolution 39 (Legislative Referendum 381, 2026 session).
  • Reported votes: House 80-14 (Apr 15, 2026); Senate 40-8 (Apr 27, 2026).
  • Placed on the November 3, 2026 general-election ballot as State Question 847.

Who Supports This

  • Rep. Kyle Hilbert (R-29), author
  • The legislative majorities that referred it (House 80-14, Senate 40-8)

Who Opposes This

  • Oklahoma Policy Institute (warns of cuts to public-education and career-tech funding)

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is property-tax relief with a real cost attached, and the amendment is honest about neither hiding it. Slowing how fast a home's taxable value can climb protects families — especially fixed-income seniors — from being taxed out of homes they already own. That is a genuine good. The other side of the ledger is just as real: the same cap that shields the homeowner also starves the school district, county, and career-tech that run on those dollars, which is why the Oklahoma Policy Institute opposes it. A citizen weighing this is really weighing who absorbs the squeeze — the homeowner or the local budget. There's no free version.
