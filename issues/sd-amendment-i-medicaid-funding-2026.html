South Dakota Constitutional Amendment I — Medicaid Expansion Conditioned on Federal Funding (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All South Dakota Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Lets the state discontinue the expanded Medicaid coverage that voters added to the constitution in 2022 if the federal medical-assistance match drops below 90 percent (the current rate).
  • Does not by itself end coverage — it creates the constitutional authority to do so if Washington stops paying its share.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in House Joint Resolution 5001 (2025 session).
  • Reported votes: House 59-7 (Jan 21, 2025); Senate 31-3 (Mar 3, 2025).
  • Placed on the November 3, 2026 general-election ballot as Amendment I.

Who Supports This

  • Legislative majorities that referred it (House 59-7, Senate 31-3)

Who Opposes This

  • Medicaid-expansion advocates who warn it threatens coverage for current enrollees

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is a budget guardrail, not a values fight. The 2022 expansion is written into the constitution, so the state is on the hook even if federal support shrinks; this gives lawmakers a release valve if Washington stops paying its 90 percent. Supporters call that simple prudence — don't promise coverage the state can't fund alone. Critics hear a trapdoor under tens of thousands of enrollees. The honest question for a voter is who should carry the risk if federal money dries up: the state treasury or the people on the rolls. Count the cost either way.
