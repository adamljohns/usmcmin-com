South Dakota Constitutional Amendment K — Unclaimed Property Trust Fund (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All South Dakota Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Creates a 'trust for unclaimed property fund' in the state treasury.
  • Net receipts from unclaimed property — dormant bank accounts, uncashed checks, unclaimed insurance payouts, safe-deposit contents — flow into the trust after claims and expenses.
  • Beginning July 1, 2027, a portion of the interest and income is distributed to the general fund, leaving the principal intact.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment carried in Senate Joint Resolution 505 (2025 session).
  • Reported votes: Senate 35-0 (Feb 20, 2025); House 69-0 (Mar 6, 2025) — unanimous in both chambers.
  • Placed on the November 3, 2026 general-election ballot as Amendment K.

Who Supports This

  • Unanimous legislative majorities (Senate 35-0, House 69-0)

Who Opposes This

  • No organized opposition documented

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This is housekeeping, and it passed without a single 'no' vote. It parks dormant-account money in a trust and spends only the interest rather than the principal — the conservative instinct of living off the yield instead of raiding the corpus. Low stakes, sound stewardship. There's not much to debate here.
