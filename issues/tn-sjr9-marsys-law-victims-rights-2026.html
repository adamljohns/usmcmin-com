November 3, 2026 On the 2026 Ballot

Tennessee SJR 9 — Marsy's Law Crime Victims' Rights Amendment (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Tennessee Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Adds expanded, enumerated crime-victim rights to the state constitution (the Marsy's Law framework).
  • Provides victims greater involvement in the judicial process — notice, presence, and the right to be heard — before and after conviction.
  • Would make Tennessee roughly the 15th state to adopt Marsy's Law, and the first new Marsy's Law on a state ballot since 2020.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment (Senate Joint Resolution 9) that cleared two consecutive General Assemblies.
  • Verified second-passage votes: Senate 27-5 (Mar 3, 2025); House 93-0 (Apr 21, 2025). First-passage (2023-24) counts were not independently confirmed for this entry.
  • To pass, 'yes' votes must exceed 50 percent of the total votes cast for governor on November 3, 2026.

Who Supports This

  • Marsy's Law for All (national organization)
  • Near-unanimous legislative majorities (Senate 27-5, House 93-0)

Who Opposes This

  • Five Senate Democrats voted no; defense-bar critics nationally warn of due-process tension

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

Marsy's Law has passed in many states; Tennessee would be about the 15th. It gives victims notice, presence, and a voice in the process — hard to argue against caring for the wronged, and the House passed it 93-0. The quiet caution defense attorneys raise nationally is that constitutional victim 'rights' can collide with a defendant's due-process rights and slow cases down. Read it as a real good with a procedural cost worth watching, not a free lunch.
