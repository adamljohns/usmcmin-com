Virginia Congressional Redistricting Amendment

Statewide — All Virginia Voters

This is a statewide constitutional amendment that would allow the Virginia General Assembly to redraw U.S. congressional district maps — overriding the bipartisan redistricting commission that voters approved in 2020.

What It Actually Does

  • Strips the bipartisan commission: In 2020, Virginia voters approved a bipartisan redistricting commission. This amendment suspends that commission and hands map-drawing power back to the Democrat-controlled General Assembly.
  • Current map: Republicans hold 5 seats, Democrats hold 6 seats (11 total).
  • Proposed new map: Under maps favored by supporters, Democrats would hold 10 of 11 seats — a gain of 4 seats.
  • Funding disparity: "Virginians for Fair Elections" (YES) has raised $49 million. "Virginians for Fair Maps" (NO) has raised $9 million.

Polling

Washington Post/Schar School poll (March 26-31, 2026): Support 52%, Oppose 47% — tight margin.

Who Supports This

  • Governor Abigail Spanberger (D)
  • Former President Barack Obama
  • U.S. Senators Tim Kaine and Mark Warner
  • NAACP Virginia

Who Opposes This

  • Former Governor Glenn Youngkin (R)
  • Former Lt. Governor Winsome Earle-Sears (R)
  • U.S. Representatives Ben Cline, Morgan Griffith, Jen Kiggans (R)
  • Republican Party of Virginia

Reformed Christian Analysis

This amendment is a power grab dressed in the language of fairness. Virginia voters in 2020 chose to take redistricting out of partisan hands. This amendment reverses that decision and concentrates map-drawing power in a Democrat-controlled legislature that has voted to expand abortion, ban firearms, promote gender ideology in schools, and suppress parental rights. Christians who value the election of godly magistrates should understand what a YES vote means: fewer representatives who share their values in Congress.
