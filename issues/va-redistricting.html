Virginia: Congressional Redistricting Amendment

State of Virginia

This amendment would allow the Virginia General Assembly to redraw congressional districts outside of the normal post-census redistricting cycle, specifically in response to mid-decade redistricting actions taken by other states.

The April 21, 2026 election is the first statewide ballot measure of the 2026 cycle. The amendment addresses growing concerns about partisan gerrymandering being conducted in other states that may disadvantage Virginia's representation.

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

Redistricting is a matter of fair representation. Christians should evaluate whether this gives the legislature too much power to redraw maps for partisan advantage, or whether it is a reasonable response to other states' actions.
