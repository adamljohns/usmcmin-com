Wisconsin AJR 10 — Prohibit Closing Places of Worship in Emergencies (Nov 2026)

Statewide — All Wisconsin Voters

What The Amendment Does

  • Prohibits the state and local governments from ordering places of worship closed during a declared state of emergency.
  • Explicitly includes public-health emergencies within that prohibition.

How It Got On The Ballot

  • Legislatively referred constitutional amendment (Assembly Joint Resolution 10). Wisconsin has no citizen initiative; an amendment must pass two consecutive legislatures, then go to the voters.
  • Reported second-passage votes: Assembly 56-43; Senate 18-15 (Jan 21, 2026). First-passage tallies in the prior legislature were not independently confirmed for this entry.
  • Certified for the November 3, 2026 general-election ballot.

Who Supports This

  • Rep. Ron Tusler (R-3) and most Assembly and Senate Republicans, joined by two Assembly Democrats

Who Opposes This

  • Most Democrats, including Sen. Chris Larson (D-7), who argued it prevents public-health officers from doing their jobs

RESOLUTE Citizen Analysis

This grows directly out of 2020, when some governments kept liquor stores and big-box retailers open while houses of worship were ordered shut. Supporters say worship is not a hobby the state may switch off in a crisis — it is a first-freedom that should not be treated as less essential than a hardware store. Opponents, like Sen. Chris Larson, warn that a flat ban ties the hands of public-health officials in a true emergency. The question for a voter: when the next emergency comes, should worship be essential by default, or negotiated case-by-case? The Body is told plainly not to forsake assembling.

"Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the manner of some is."
