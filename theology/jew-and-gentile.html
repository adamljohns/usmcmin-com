Jew & Gentile

In Christ, the dividing wall of hostility is permanently abolished.

Reformed Covenant Theology — Fulfillment / Expansion

Jew & Gentile Olive Tree

Definition

Jew: /dʒuː/ — Gentile: /ˈdʒɛn.taɪl/

Jew from Hebrew Yehudi (of Judah). Gentile from Latin gentilis (of a clan or nation), translating Hebrew goy (nation) and Greek ethnos (people-group).

Historically, "Jew" refers to the physical descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob; "Gentile" refers to all other nations.

📘 Biblical Definition

The historical Jew-Gentile distinction was established by God under the Old Covenant to separate a holy nation (Exodus 19:5-6) through whom the Messiah would come. Gentiles were "strangers from the covenants of promise" (Ephesians 2:12).

However, in the New Covenant, this redemptive distinction is permanently abolished. Christ broke down the dividing wall of hostility, creating "one new man in place of the two" (Ephesians 2:15). Today, a true Jew is one inwardly, circumcised in heart by the Spirit (Romans 2:29). Both ethnic Jews and Gentiles who belong to Christ are Abraham's seed and heirs according to the promise (Galatians 3:29).

📝 Webster 1828 Definition

JEW: A Hebrew or Israelite.

GENTILE: A pagan; one who worships false gods; any person not a Jew.

📘 Key Scripture

For no one is a Jew who is merely one outwardly, nor is circumcision outward and physical. But a Jew is one inwardly, and circumcision is circumcision of the heart by the Spirit, not by the written code. Such a person's praise is not from man but from God.

— Romans 2:28–29 (ESV)

For he himself is our peace, who has made us both one and has broken down in his flesh the dividing wall of hostility by abolishing the law of commandments expressed in ordinances, that he might create in himself one new man in place of the two, so making peace.

— Ephesians 2:14–15 (ESV)

There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is no male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus. And if you are Christ's, then you are Abraham's offspring, heirs according to promise.

— Galatians 3:28–29 (ESV)

⚠ Modern Corruption

Two major errors obscure the biblical truth.

Dispensationalism commits the error of division, falsely teaching a "two-track" salvation system with separate futures for ethnic Israel and the Church. This violates the Law of Non-Contradiction: two things cannot be entirely unified into "one new man" (Ephesians 2:15) while simultaneously maintaining separate covenantal destinies.

Replacement Theology (Supersessionism) teaches that God chopped down the tree of Israel and planted a new Gentile Church. This misunderstands God's covenant faithfulness.

The Reformed, biblical truth is Covenant Fulfillment: God has only ever had one unified people. The Church has not replaced Israel; rather, believing Gentiles have been grafted into true Israel, while unbelieving ethnic Jews were broken off due to unbelief. God's promises to Israel are not nullified in the Church; they are definitively fulfilled in Christ, who is the true Seed of Abraham.

Usage

  • "The gospel is to the Jew first, and also to the Greek, as both share the exact same desperate need for the exact same Savior."
